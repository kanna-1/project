There's no one-size-fits-all social media strategy. But if I *had* to give one, I would say...

(1) Start where your audience is AND where they're likely to convert.

Your target audience might be on Instagram — but they might *not* be thinking about the problem your product solves. If you're marketing a B2B SaaS product, there's a solid chance your customers aren't scrolling Instagram, hoping to make a buying decision on your product.

You might find you ought to spend more time on LinkedIn or YouTube.

(2) Focus on this platform exclusively until you gain traction.

Check out the popular content in your niche. Do some audience research in SparkToro. Get a feel for what your audience cares about. Create content for them. Keep testing different content types and see what's resonating.

(3) Once you gain traction and have a repeatable process for content creation, expand to your next social media channel.

Of course... there's so much more nuance to all of this.

Every business is different.

Every marketing team has different strengths.

And audiences’ needs are changing faster than ever.

How are you keeping up?

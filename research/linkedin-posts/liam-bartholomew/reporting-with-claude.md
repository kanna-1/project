I used to dread the last week of every month.

Not because of the work. Because of the reporting.

In a previous life, our marketing team could easily burn a week a month just on it. 

Pulling data, building decks, training people to add commentary that actually made sense. Back and forth. Huge reports. And honestly, limited insight at the end of it.

The problem was never the intention. Regular reporting is good practice.

Good reporting is extremely hard to do well. Especially in small teams where the same person writing the creative brief is also expected to be data literate enough to surface the next big insight.

That's changed for me.

I connected our CRM directly into Claude. 

What used to be hours trawling through filters, messing with views, hoping the data was even correct, now takes minutes. I can query our pipeline and get automated reports I actually trust and told where data is missing too. 

Things I'm looking to have set up from the start:

- Paid and SEO reporting, fully automated. The time gets spent on commentary and decisions, not pulling numbers.
- Project management tools queried directly. No more chasing the team for manual activity updates. I used to really hate doing those.
- Pipeline and revenue data on demand. No more waiting for end of month to know what's actually moving.

And this isn't just for small teams. If anything, bigger teams have more to gain. More tools, more data, more complexity to untangle.

1 week back every month doesn't sound like much.

But that's time back on the campaign that keeps getting pushed. The content series you never quite launched. The test you've been meaning to run for three months.

That's what good reporting should do. Not just tell you what happened. Free you up to change what happens next.

We closed out February at $1.8M MRR (-0.2%), our 6th down month ever. We also (accidentally) launched R!B2B 5 days ago and have 10 paying customers and $4.2K MRR. Here’s what SUCKED and was AWESOME in February: 

First, what SUCKED:

At Retention.com, activity still feels slow from the holiday season, cancellations are high, and we saw an uptick in competition for parts of our core offering. 

That sounds terrible, and I’m sure if you’re rooting against us, you’ll enjoy the next several months as we figure out how to navigate through this operating environment which is decidedly more difficult than it was a year ago.

One thing I can see from looking at BuiltWith is that many of the rising stars in the Shopify ecosystem are experiencing a similar malaise - some severe.

I’m just the only idiot writing about it publicly!

While we’ve had trouble growing topline the last 60 days, we have continued to improve bottom line, and we’ll be in a position to resume growth in the Q2.

What was AWESOME:

We generated $624,132 of free cash in February, which was our 2nd highest month ever.

We also accidentally launched rb2b.com - our person-level website visitor ID product - a week ago and got 1,000 free signups without even intending to. But, we only have 10 paying customers. Not a great conversion rate.

There is a LONG road ahead of refining our PLG motion, which either currently doesn’t exist or is all wrong. 

My emotional experience has gone from euphoria to disillusionment as the excitement we built over the past six months was met with a product that lacks the key features and integrations we need for paying customers to find value.

On the bright side, our top-of-funnel has been excellent. 

Santosh and I have done great generating meetings and signups on LinkedIn, our cold email effort that Taylor is spearheading has had great results.

Also, our plan to have AI chatbots handle the bulk of support requests is working (here's a few staggering early numbers): 

In the past week, we had 1293 instances of users seeking support:

- 1230 (95.1%) were resolved by ‘self-serve support’
- 28 (2.2%) were resolved by a human straight out of the gate
- 35 (2.7%) started self-serve but needed human help

That's nuts!

We still have LOTS of work to do to help people along the activation journey, and some pricing changes and messaging to inform people of what they can pay for and why. Plus, building the features (mainly filtering for ICP contacts) and integrations (mainly Salesforce and Hubspot) that we’re missing, but...

We'll get there.

_

Success doesn’t happen in a straight line, as you’re witnessing now.

However, if we keep our heads down and keep pursuing excellence we’ll get through this uncomfortable period. 

I’m sure some of you are absolutely crushing it out there, and to you, I tip my hat. 

For the rest of you, I just want you to know that you’re not alone. 

What we do is freaking hard. 

Keep your head up. 

And keep building.
